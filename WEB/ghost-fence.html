What is a "Bounce"?

A bounce is recorded when an elephant exhibits a sharp turning angle within the fence buffer, moving towards the boundary and then abruptly retreating. It indicates a behavioral response to the historical or "ghost" boundary, where the spatial memory of the physical fence continues to act as a mental barrier.

Insights from the Interim Phase

During the Interim Phase (Dec 9, 2023 - Feb 9, 2024), spatial memory continued to strongly influence movement patterns despite the removal of the physical boundary. As individuals gradually expanded into novel territory, their habitat selection and behavioral dynamics adjusted to the altered landscape.

Elephant E4 (Half Moon) exhibited pronounced "Ghost Fence" interactions. Despite the physical boundary removal, her trajectory shows repeated "bounces" along the former fenceline prior to crossing into the novel territory. This rapid exploratory behavior contrasts with E3, who exhibited a 60-day latency period before entering the newly accessible area.
